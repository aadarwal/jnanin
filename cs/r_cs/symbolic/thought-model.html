My Thinking Model

Tracking Thought Evolution

This page represents an experiment in documenting my cognitive evolution. By tracking changes in my essays over time and documenting the reasoning behind those changes, I'm creating a dataset that reveals patterns in how my thinking evolves. This project has two goals:

  1. Self-reflection: Understanding my own thought patterns, biases, and evolution
  2. AI modeling: Creating a structured dataset that could potentially train a model to emulate my reasoning process

The visualization below shows the network of concepts across my essays and how they've evolved over time. Larger nodes represent concepts that have undergone significant evolution through multiple revisions.

Identified Thought Patterns

Initial Western-Centric Framing → Broader Cultural Context
I often start with frameworks derived from Western philosophy or political theory, then gradually incorporate broader cultural contexts as my understanding deepens.
Examples: Mandate of Heaven essay (v1→v2), Liberal Arts Education (v1→v3)
Observed in 4 of 6 tracked essays
Technology as Afterthought → Central Consideration
Technological dimensions are frequently absent in my initial analyses but become increasingly central in later revisions as I recognize their fundamental importance.
Examples: Mandate of Heaven (v2→v3), Future essay (v1→v2)
Observed in 3 of 6 tracked essays
Abstract Theorizing → Concrete Examples
I tend to start with abstract theoretical frameworks, then incorporate more concrete examples and case studies in subsequent revisions.
Examples: Bell Labs x DARPA essay (all versions), Ship of Theseus (v1→v2)
Observed in 5 of 6 tracked essays
Binary Oppositions → Nuanced Spectrum
Initial versions often frame concepts as binary oppositions, while later versions recognize more complex spectra and interdependencies.
Examples: Mandate of Heaven (v2→v3), Philosophy essay (v1→v2)
Observed in 4 of 6 tracked essays
